M6_RUIN_CONGESTED__H_WINDOW_CLOSURE

Ruin (CONGESTED): selects bursts on weapons with the least free time (uses free_time_per_weapon(sol)), i.e., weapons that are most tightly scheduled; removes q bursts where q = ruin_count_from_val(val). This targets bottlenecked weapons to free schedule space around congested slots.
Rebuild (H_WINDOW_CLOSURE): build_candidates enumerates feasible commits; the heuristic greedily chooses candidates with smallest slack = window_end - candidate_time (earliest window-closing first), breaking ties by earliest time then survival/target threat. Rebuild loop uses apply_one_arm_commit_inc to commit incrementally.
When to use / benefit: use when many bursts cluster causing missed or tight windows; helps shift shots to close imminent windows.
Drawbacks: may remove useful assignments on congested weapons; rebuild may prefer urgency over long-term threat reduction.
M7_RUIN_LOW_MARGINAL__H_COVER_FIRST

Ruin (LOW_MARGINAL_PAIR): at pair-level: score each (wid,tid) by sum of marginal contributions (using marginal_uncommit_increase); remove weakest pairs (all their bursts). Targets low marginal-value structure.
Rebuild (H_COVER_FIRST): chooses targets with fewest feasible weapons (count_target_feasible_weapons) first, and prefers weapons that can cover scarce targets; tie-break by weapon action counts and survival. Commits incrementally.
When to use / benefit: good when low-value assignments are crowding scarce targets and you need to reallocate capacity to under-served (scarce) targets.
Drawbacks: may remove low-marginal bursts that are tactically necessary for feasibility; pair-level removal is coarse.
M8_RUIN_CONGESTED__H_BACKLOG_RELIEF

Ruin (CONGESTED): same congested selection as M6 — remove bursts from weapons with little free time.
Rebuild (H_BACKLOG_RELIEF): picks a weapon with the largest backlog (most feasible actions) then selects the best candidate for that weapon (max survival). The idea is to reduce action queue pressure on overloaded weapons.
When to use / benefit: relieves overloaded weapons so later commits can use their windows more flexibly.
Drawbacks: may shift load elsewhere creating new bottlenecks; focusing on backlog can deprioritize high-threat targets.
M10_RUIN_RANDOM__H_SPREAD_THEN_FOCUS

Ruin (RANDOM_PAIR): removes entire random (wid,tid) pairs — introduces large structural diversity.
Rebuild (H_SPREAD_THEN_FOCUS): phased strategy via ctx.phase: early phase favors spreading (prefer unengaged targets), mid-phase maximizes survival, late-phase prioritizes slack (urgent windows). Phase determined by remaining ammo context when selecting (apply_one_arm_commit_inc sets ctx.phase).
When to use / benefit: exploratory move to escape deep local minima; spreading early avoids prematurely focusing on the same targets.
Drawbacks: high randomness — may remove good structure; performance depends on phase scheduling and amount of ruin.
M11_RUIN_CONGESTED__H_ANTI_BOTTLENECK

Ruin (CONGESTED): remove bursts from weapons with low free time to relieve congestion.
Rebuild (H_ANTI_BOTTLENECK): selects the weapon with the latest next-feasible-fire-time (largest min candidate t), then chooses the best candidate for that weapon, aiming to unblock weapons that otherwise delay scheduling.
When to use / benefit: when bottleneck weapons are delaying assignments; helps push the schedule to use earlier opportunities elsewhere.
Drawbacks: may focus too narrowly on a weapon while neglecting global objective; identification of bottleneck via min-t can mislead if windows differ widely.
M12_RUIN_OVER_COVERED__H_OPPORTUNITY_LOCK

Ruin (OVER_COVERED): removes bursts from targets with many shots (shots_per_target(sol)), i.e., over-covered targets.
Rebuild (H_OPPORTUNITY_LOCK): uses ctx.prev_target_feasible_weapons to detect where feasible weapon counts have dropped and prefers moves that “lock in” scarce opportunities — tie-breaks by time and survival/threat. It tries to secure windows that are about to lose feasibility.
When to use / benefit: when you suspect over-allocation to some targets and want to free resources for targets that risk losing feasible weapons.
Drawbacks: relies on accurate prev-feasible counts; may remove redundant coverage that provided robustness, reducing resilience to later constraints.
M13_RUIN_LOW_MARGINAL__H_MARGINAL_OBJECTIVE_DROP

Ruin (LOW_MARGINAL_PAIR): pair-level removal of weakest pairs by summed marginal_uncommit_increase.
Rebuild (H_MARGINAL_OBJECTIVE_DROP): chooses candidates maximizing marginal_objective_drop(sol, c) = surv * threat * p — i.e., largest immediate benefit to objective. Commits highest marginal contributors first.
When to use / benefit: seeks quick objective improvements by rebuilding using the highest marginal-return shots.
Drawbacks: greedy marginal maximization can be myopic (ignores interaction/slack), and pair-level ruin may still remove useful structural links.
M14_RUIN_OVER_COVERED__H_KILL_CHAIN_FINISHER

Ruin (OVER_COVERED): remove bursts from heavily-shot targets.
Rebuild (H_KILL_CHAIN_FINISHER): prefers targets already engaged (complete kill chains) — among those picks highest marginal drop; breaks ties by scarcity, time, and survival. Intention is to finish targets efficiently once engagement started.
When to use / benefit: when finishing engaged targets yields good bang-for-buck (reduces residual threat effectively).
Drawbacks: can over-focus on finishing already-engaged low-threat targets instead of spreading damage to higher-threat ones.
M15_RUIN_CONGESTED__H_FUTURE_FLEX_PRESERVER

Ruin (CONGESTED): same congested selection mechanism.
Rebuild (H_FUTURE_FLEX_PRESERVER): chooses candidates by marginal drop minus lambda/(1+weapon_action_count) penalization — discourages filling already-busy weapons to preserve future flexibility.
When to use / benefit: maintains long-run option value, avoids overloading weapons that would reduce future feasible choices.
Drawbacks: introduces a smoothing bias; may pass up high immediate benefit to keep flexibility, hurting short-term objective.
M16_RUIN_RANDOM__H_BASELINE_TWO_STAGE

Ruin (RANDOM_PAIR): removes whole random (wid,tid) pairs.
Rebuild (H_BASELINE_TWO_STAGE): exact two-stage lexicographic rule: Stage 1 pick target with highest survival*threat (tie by threat, then window_end); Stage 2 for that target pick weapon with fewest feasible targets (scarcity), then by pij (probability), earliest time, wid. This encodes a conservative, structured baseline policy.
When to use / benefit: reliable fallback rebuild that balances stage-wise priorities and scarcity; useful as a stabilizing rebuild to recover decent structure after random ruin.
Drawbacks: rigid lexicographic priorities can miss nuanced tradeoffs; less adaptive than continuous-score heuristics.

M2_RUIN_RANDOM__H_SURV_THREAT_TIE
Ruin: RANDOM_PAIR

this introduces broad structural diversity while keeping bursts grouped by pair
Rebuild: H_SURV_THREAT_TIE
chooses candidate commits by best target survival first
if two candidates give the same survival, it breaks ties by higher threat
then by earlier fire time, lower weapon id, lower target id
Why use it:
good when the current solution may be stuck in a local minimum and you want a general shuffle
it preserves promising pairs by rebuilding with a “survival-first, threat-aware” policy
Drawback:
randomness can discard useful structure

M4_RUIN_OVER_COVERED__H_EXCLUSIVE_RESERVE

it tries to eliminate over-allocation to saturated targets
Rebuild: H_EXCLUSIVE_RESERVE

otherwise it allows all feasible candidates
among allowed candidates it selects the one with best survival
Why use it:
useful when some targets are “over-shot” and other targets are in danger of being left with only one feasible weapon
it preserves scarce opportunities for exclusive targets
Drawback:

can be too conservative if the exclusivity condition is not actually the critical constraint

Why use it:
useful when the best local improvement is exchanging two assignments rather than moving just one
captures trade-offs where each weapon is better suited to the other target
Drawback:
pairwise scanning is quadratic in active pairs, so it can be costly
still local: cannot fix deeper structural issues that require more than one swap
